## Executive Summary

Firefox has improved to **115 failing subtests** as of 2026-04-26, down 12 from the April 15 report (127) and 15 from the April 14 baseline (130). The primary driver is Byron Campen's April 23 landing of [1339203](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1339203) (rtcpMuxPolicy), which resolved 14 previously failing subtests. Safari has made a substantial leap in the 242 preview — its failing count dropped from 27 to 9, while Chrome remains flat at 78. Mozilla is still furthest from zero, but with multiple active fixes in flight — H264 (jib), extmap-allow-mixed (Baker), RTCAudioSourceStats (Grunbaum) — and the large sdpFmtpLine and RTCTransportStats clusters still addressable, there is a realistic path to meaningful score improvement over the next quarter. One scope note: vendor consensus is forming to remove the h264-profile-levels tests from Interop 2026, which would reduce the Firefox burndown by an additional 5 points without any browser work.
