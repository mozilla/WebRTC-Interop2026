## Executive Summary

Firefox dropped from **70 to 60** failing tracked subtests over this three-week reporting period. The main driver was the `RTCPeerConnection-mandatory-getStats.https.html` test moving from 64/73 to 71/73 (+7 subtests), unlocked by three related landings: [2019349](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=2019349) (bytesSent/bytesReceived, **Philipp Hancke** from Google — the first external contribution), [2019333](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=2019333) (localCertificateId/remoteCertificateId, **anna.weine**), and a cascade effect that also passed the RTCCertificateStats fields (base64Certificate, fingerprint, fingerprintAlgorithm) that Bugzilla still tracks under a NEW bug. Byron Campen's [1584318](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1584318) (RTCRtcpParameters.cname/reducedSize) cleared `RTCRtpParameters-rtcp.html` (0/2 → 2/2), removing the last prerequisite for the headerExtensions cluster he is now working on ([1765851](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1765851)). Nico Grunbaum filed and fixed [2051003](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=2051003) in the same cycle, closing the `rtp-payloadtypes` gap that had been carried without a Firefox bug for six weeks. Meta-bug closed share now stands at **51%** (23 of 45 child bugs). Firefox's single-browser lead over Chrome grew to 14 subtests. Safari regressed +2 on crypto-suite after a WPT-side cipher-suite cleanup ([wpt#60663](https://github.com/web-platform-tests/wpt/pull/60663)); Firefox unaffected.
